The end of a journey is also the start of a new journey

Older: A nice LaTeX template

At around 6 o’clock on a dewy day of November 2020, the phone rang on my way home. I could not reply since I was driving, so almost one hour later did I decide to call back.

“Are you Nguyen Dinh Tien?”, said the girl on the other end.

“Yes”, I replied. Normally I would expect professionals to declare on behalf of what organization are they speaking, but this time was quite different. I gave myself in an interview of some sort of physics society–or a club if you wish–recently, so this must be them, I thought.

Hai Anh briefly asked about my agenda, and if I could attend the school fair with the club. The invitation was more like a spurs of the moment thing because the fair was supposed to be held the very next week, and from what I remember from the interview preparation is far from completed. However, it’s always a good thing if you could start from scratch and build up everything from there: you are likely to make mistakes, but that is precisely what you need to grow.

“OK. See you on Friday!” she concluded.

I would not describe what I felt was excitement. But it would be lying to deny the eagerness.

.

HUS Physics Club (HPC) resurrected itself from hibernation on 29 November 2020 when the club made its first appearance at the Maths Open Day. A group of four students was appointed to be the club’s leaders not long after that, and we soon geared up for our next mission.
