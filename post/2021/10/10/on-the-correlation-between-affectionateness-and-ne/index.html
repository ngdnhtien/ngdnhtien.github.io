On the correlation between affectionateness and N(e)

Newer: Nhà báo chín điểm
Older: UPhysicsC 2021–We study Mars for 48 hours

Abstract

We investigate the likelihood of girls being into their texting partners predicating on the number of ending characters $N(e)$ they typically send in a message.

Premise

So I am having a crush on a girl and we have been texting for a while now. Most of the time she would demonstrate her excitement to my statement by increasing the number of ending characters in a sentence. For example, “Vânggggg”, or “Anh là nhấtttt”.

I conceive this as a positive sign of an uncoming intimate relationship (future-spoiler: we departed). So I’d like to conduct an experiment with my friend to see how many words a girl would send if she is into the man.

Experiment

Let us denote the number of ending character(s) of a specific word as $N(e)$

For instance: “Okelaaa”, in this case $N(e)=3$.

A vast amount of time spent, numerous methods were used, including:

  1. Spontaneously typing Okela

In this experiment, we typed more than 100 times the word Okela spontaneously. We observe that, on average, a person with the intention to increase the length of “Okela” will likely to type a total of 3 “a”(s).

  1. Asking experts in the field

Check out validate, truthful online resources, e.g: Muong14.vn.

Conclusion

I have reached a conclusion: The affectionateness a person has for you correlates positively with $N(e)$. To be precise:

  • If $N(e)<2$, you’re dead.
  • If $3\leq N(e)\leq$, well, almost there!
  • If $N(e)\leq 5$, propose now. She’d say yes.
  • $N(e)=2$ is ridiculously rare. She would have probably either crazy or her phone got damaged beforehand.
