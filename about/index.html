About

I’m Nguyễn Đình Tiến. If you’re not falimiar with Vietnamese pronounciation, just simply call me Heisenberg—that’s just fine. No, not the physicist one, the Alburquerque methamphetamine kingpin one.

I was born in Hue, and raised in Binh Duong. I lived in Ho Chi Minh City for a while, before moving to Hanoi. For those who wonder, recent survey indicates that the majority of my friends believes I speak Southern accent, though imo my active vocabulary spans the three regions.

Academic

I was once a student of the Programme de Formation d’Ingénieurs d’ Excellence au Vietnam–PFIEV at Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology, before leaping over and to become a physics student at VNU University of Science.

At the moment, I am a part of Dr Hung Q. Nguyen’s group. My research center around the microwave control and characterization of superconducting circuits consisting of Josephson tunnel junctions for information processing in higher-dimensional non-linear oscillators.
